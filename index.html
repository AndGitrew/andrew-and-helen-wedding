Welcome to the official Safety rulebook.

SAFETY - the game

Created by Marc Bernier, Andrew and Brandon Liston

GAME SUMMARY:

Safety consists of a lineup of players each taking turns adding to the team's points by either making a free throw or jumping and making their own rebound. If, however, a point is not made, then the player must save or 'safety' the game by making a basket from where the ball was caught without violating the bounce or the step rule. The object of the game is to beat a previous high score.

THE RULES:

RULE #1

Shooting lines:

  1. Standard - 20 feet away from the basketball net.
  2. Supershot - 40 feet away from the basketball net.

RULE #2

The basketball net should be 7 feet from the ground.

RULE #3

The game can start when a free throw point is made from the standard shooting line.

RULE #4

Points are made by either:

  1. Making a successful free throw from the standard shooting line.
  2. Scoring a rebound before a bounce by catching and shooting the ball with one's feet off the ground (an unlimited number of these rebounds are permitted to score a single point).

RULE #5

All successul shots except safety shots add one point to the team's score.

RULE #6

Although it doesn't count as a point, the game can be saved with a 'safety':

  1. Safety - following an unsuccessful point attempt, the shooter has only one chance to sink the ball from where the ball was caught.
  2. Supershot - after an unsuccesful safety shot, if the team has previously earned a Supershot, the shooter must sink a shot from the Supershot line 40 feet away. Supershots can regenerate after the team's score reaches a multiple of 10.

RULE #7

The Bounce Rule - One bounce is allowed before a safety attempt.

RULE #8

The Step Rule - A one step shot, layup, or dunk is permitted to make a safety.

RULE #9

The game ends when:

  1. The team misses a safety and/or a supershot.
  2. The ball bounces more than once.
  3. Someone shoots an airball on his/her initial shot.
